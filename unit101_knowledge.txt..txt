Assessment Criteria
1.1 Identify relevant organisational health and safety procedures

The main legislation is the Health and Safety at Work Act 1974. This act outlines the responsibilities of employers and employees to ensure workplace health, safety, and welfare.
Employers must:

Conduct risk assessments to identify hazards and control risks.
Provide training and information to staff.
Maintain safe equipment and premises.
Provide Personal Protective Equipment (PPE) where necessary.


Employees must:

Follow health and safety procedures.
Use equipment safely.
Report hazards and incidents.


Other relevant legislation:

Electricity at Work Regulations 1989
Computer Misuse Act 1990
Management of Health and Safety at Work Regulations 1999
Provision and Use of Work Equipment Regulations NI 1999
Display Screen Equipment Regulations 1992
Reporting of Injuries, Diseases and Dangerous Occurrences Regulations 2013



1.2 Identify available sources of health and safety information

Health and Safety Executive (HSE) website: http://www.hse.gov.uk/index.htm
Company handbook
Company intranet
Noticeboards and workplace signage
Training sessions and documentation

1.3 Demonstrate how relevant health and safety procedures have been followed

Provide practical evidence, such as:

Completed risk assessments (using the official template)
Email chains or screenshots showing reporting of health and safety incidents
Examples of following procedures (e.g., hazard reports, workplace adjustments)


Evidence should be specific to your workplace and role.
Screenshots, printouts, or email exchanges are encouraged as supporting evidence.
